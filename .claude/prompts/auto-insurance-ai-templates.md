## Template 3: State Requirements

### AI Prompt
```
Explain minimum auto insurance requirements in {{state}}.

REQUIREMENTS:
- List exact state minimum coverage limits (bodily injury per person/accident, property damage)
- Explain if {{state}} is a fault or no-fault state
- Mention penalties for driving without insurance in {{state}}
- Compare minimum vs recommended coverage
- Mention SR-22 requirements if applicable
- Note any {{state}}-specific requirements

FOCUS: Legal minimums but hint that more coverage may be wise
LENGTH: 150-200 words
```

### Output Example
```text
California law requires all drivers to carry minimum liability insurance coverage of 15/30/5. This means:

• $15,000 for bodily injury or death per person
• $30,000 total for bodily injury or death per accident  
• $5,000 for property damage per accident

California is a "fault" state, meaning the driver responsible for an accident pays for damages through their insurance. While these minimums satisfy legal requirements, most insurance experts recommend higher limits of at least 100/300/50 for better protection, especially given Los Angeles's high accident rates and costly vehicle repairs.

Driving without insurance in California results in serious penalties:
• First offense: $100-$200 fine plus penalty assessments
• License suspension until proof of insurance is provided
• Possible vehicle impoundment
• SR-22 filing requirement for 3 years

California also requires insurers to offer uninsured motorist coverage, which we strongly recommend accepting given that approximately 15% of California drivers lack insurance—well above the national average.
```
